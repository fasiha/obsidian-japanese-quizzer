# Must-Know Counters

The 17 most essential Japanese counters: the two that appear in nearly every situation (個, 本) and the seventeen that any intermediate learner needs to recognize and produce reliably.

個 (こ) — Anything with a distinct shape. Examples: Pretty much anything with a distinct shape or boundary..
<details><summary>Vocab</summary>
- counter:こ
</details>

本 (ほん) — Long, cylindrical things. Examples: Pens, pencils, asparagus, soba, darts, firewood, trees, bamboo, cords, threads, dumplings, water wells, injections, folding fans, eels, tails, nail clippers, icicles, fishing rods, film, chimneys, tenugui, trains, sashes, telephone poles, cylindrical batteries, bottles, tires, ribbons, cacti, soda cans.
<details><summary>Vocab</summary>
- counter:ほん
</details>

枚 (まい) — Flat things. Examples: Paper, photos, rafts, bath mats, shells, playing cards, credit cards, t-shirts, pants, other clothes, dust cloths, walls, a single serving of soba, a single serving of gyoza.
<details><summary>Vocab</summary>
- counter:まい
</details>

匹 (ひき) — Animals, bugs, fish, monsters that don't like humans. Examples: Dogs, cats, monkeys, fish, prawns, shrimps, lobsters, wolves, rabbits (unless they're counted with 羽), mosquitoes, shellfish, deer, worms, caterpillars, silkworms, earthworms, frogs, snails, crabs, tortoises, turtles, octopuses, animals, insects, dragonflies, sea otters, ogres, monsters, naughty children, animal-like people.
<details><summary>Vocab</summary>
- counter:ひき
</details>

頭 (とう) — Large animals, farm animals. Examples: Cows, horses, livestock, elephants, gorillas, whales, camels, tigers, "professional" animals, insects on display, animals for sale.

- counter:とう
</details>

羽 (わ) — Birds, rabbits. Examples: Chickens, ostriches, peacocks, penguins, other birds, also rabbits, origami cranes.

冊 (さつ) — Books. Examples: Books, book collections, albums, notebooks, memo pads, musical scores, catalogs, magazines, dictionaries, publications, documents, booklets.
